About the Province

The Mystical Island of Fire

Location: Nestled in the Central Visayas region, Siquijor floats southeast of Cebu and Negros, over the clear northern channels of Mindanao Sea.

History: Dubbed Isla del Fuego by Spanish colonizers due to the brilliant glow of massive firefly clusters clinging to molave tree forests.

Known For: Multi-layered turquoise springs, centuries-old banyan trees, historical Spanish brick masonry, and folk healing traditions.

Culture: Celebrated for the unique Folk Healing Festival during Holy Week, where artisans gather organic mountain barks and roots under full moon.

Island Celebrations

Festivals & Culture

September
Annual Charter Celebration
Solili Festival
Siquijor Town, Siquijor

The crown jewel of Siquijor's charter week celebrations. Dancers perform the rhythmic, fluid movements of the Solili courtship ritual — an elegant marriage dance honoring family unions and community ancestors.

Holy Week
Ancient Healing Tradition
Folk Healing Festival
Mt. Bandilaan, San Antonio

A mystical gathering on Mount Bandilaan's peak. Traditional medicine men, native potion shamans, and botanists compound secret natural elements to prepare ceremonial healing tonics during Holy Week.

Culinary Treasures

Food & Local Delicacies

Siquijor Torta

A sweet pastry native to the island, unique because it incorporates natural coconut wine (tuba) as a primary leavening component instead of modern commercial yeast.

Public Markets & Bakeries
Pan de Bisaya

A century-old recipe featuring thick, firm outer crust rings encasing an unbelievably soft, dense bread core. Famously dipped into hot chocolate brew arrays.

Lazi Town Bakeries
Buko Halo-Halo

A classic icy layers treat packed with tropical fruit chunks, creamy milks, and jellies, meticulously mixed and presented inside a freshly carved green coconut shell.

Traveller's Guide

Travel Tips & Reminders

Best Time to Visit

Aim for November through May (dry season months). Plan around Holy Week to witness the mystical folk healing convocations at Mt. Bandilaan.

Getting Around

Board fast ferries from Dumaguete or Bohol. Secure a rented scooter at the port gate for easy circle-island navigation at your own pace.

Safety Reminders

Crime is virtually non-existent here. Drive defensively and always wear helmets when executing night drives on the circular island highway.

Currency & ATMs

Carry sufficient Philippine Pesos (PHP). ATMs exist in Lazi and San Juan, but countryside beach vendors accept physical bills only.

Eco Responsibility

Never anchor on or step over shallow living coral inside fish sanctuaries. Always dispose of single-use plastics responsibly — leave no trace.

Connectivity

Mobile signal is available in major areas. Globe and Smart networks provide the best coverage. Download offline maps before venturing into mountain trails.
